Understanding the Comparison Table

All-In Price

Total cost to get started — evaluation fee plus any activation fee. Reflects the discounted price when using code SOPF.

Profit Target

Amount of profit you must reach during evaluation to qualify for a funded account.

Drawdown

How far your account can fall before breaching. End-of-Day updates after close. Intraday tracks unrealized P&L live. Static never moves.

P/D Ratio

Profit Target ÷ Drawdown. A lower ratio (closer to 1.0) means an easier eval. Higher means you need more profit with less room to lose.

Daily Loss Limit

Max you can lose in one trading session. Some firms have no limit. Soft breach = locked for the day. Hard breach = account failed.

Days to Pass

Minimum trading days required before your eval can be approved, even if you hit the profit target earlier.

Days to $

How quickly you can request your first payout after passing. Ranges from 1 day to 10+ trading days depending on the firm.

Profit Split

Your percentage of profits on each payout. Most firms offer 80–90%. Some give 100% on the first payout tier.

Max Funded

How many simultaneous funded accounts you can hold. Higher = more scaling potential.
